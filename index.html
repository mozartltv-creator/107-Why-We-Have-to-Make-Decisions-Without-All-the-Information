Why Do I Have to Decide Without All the Information?

Core Thesis

The need to act before having complete clarity is not a flaw in thinking — it is a structural feature of reality.

Main Answer

Decisions are required before certainty appears because action deadlines emerge faster than full knowledge becomes available.

The tension comes not from personal failure, but from responsibility meeting uncertainty.

Key Formula

Action is required before certainty appears.

This is not failure. This is the system.
